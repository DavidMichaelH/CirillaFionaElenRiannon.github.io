Proof: Symmetric Matrices Have Real Eigenvalues
Proposition: Every symmetric matrix with real entries has only real eigenvalues.

Proof:

Let \( A \) be an \( n \times n \) real symmetric matrix. We aim to show that all eigenvalues of \( A \) are real.

Consider an eigenvalue \( \lambda \) and an associated eigenvector \( \mathbf{v} \), so that:

\( A\mathbf{v} = \lambda \mathbf{v} \)

Taking the dot product with \( \mathbf{v} \), we get:

\( \mathbf{v}^T A \mathbf{v} = \lambda \mathbf{v}^T \mathbf{v} \)

Since \( A \) is symmetric, we also have:

\( \mathbf{v}^T A \mathbf{v} = (A \mathbf{v})^T \mathbf{v} = (\lambda \mathbf{v})^T \mathbf{v} = \lambda^* \mathbf{v}^T \mathbf{v} \).

Thus, we obtain:

\( \lambda \mathbf{v}^T \mathbf{v} = \lambda^* \mathbf{v}^T \mathbf{v} \).

Since \( \mathbf{v}^T \mathbf{v} \) (the squared norm of \( \mathbf{v} \)) is positive, we conclude \( \lambda = \lambda^* \), meaning \( \lambda \) is real.

Thus, all eigenvalues of \( A \) are real. \( \square \)
