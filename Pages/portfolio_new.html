Bibliometric Analysis and Topic Modeling of Probability Research

Bibliometric analysis of 20,000+ arXiv probability papers using BERTopic.

Bibliometric Analysis and Topic Modeling of Research Papers in Probability

  • The goal of this project is to gain insights into the most prevalent research topics in probability and to identify potential areas for future research.
  • This project involves conducting a bibliometric analysis of probability research papers using titles and abstracts.
  • The dataset, which includes metadata for over 20,000 research papers, was collected using the arXiv API.
  • Topic modeling was performed using BERTopic, with clustering done via HDBSCAN.
  • GPT-3.5-turbo from OpenAI was used to create additional features derived from the abstracts.
  • A corresponding blog post on Medium was authored to describe the methods and results.
  • Future work includes developing a web app that recommends topics, areas, and papers that might be of interest to researchers based on their preferences and past reading history.

Research



Overview

My research focuses on the relationship between randomness and algorithms with an eye for applications in machine learning and data science. I use methods from theoretical computer science and statistical physics to understand how small-scale interactions can influence larger system behaviors. I'm also interested in optimization within random network models. A significant part of my work in randomized algorithms considers what they can reveal about a system's noise sensitivity and robustness.
